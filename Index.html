Chapter 02 — The Backstory

Corporate taught me how systems break. AI showed me how to rebuild them.

Twenty years in corporate healthcare. Lean Certified. ITIL trained. Process improvement, operational efficiency, regulatory compliance. I got very good at making broken systems less broken.

But I also saw what those systems left out. Who they didn't see. Who they weren't designed to serve.

When AI tools exploded onto the scene, I saw an opportunity — and a problem. The opportunity: small businesses could finally afford enterprise-level optimization. The problem: AI was replicating the same biases that excluded people in the first place.

So I built The Black Prompt to do both: help businesses optimize with AI and make sure that technology sees everyone.

Chapter 06 — The Mission

Technology should see everyone.

AI is powerful. It's also biased. Systems trained on incomplete data replicate the gaps they were built on.

When technology doesn't see you, you have to build the mirror yourself.

That's why a portion of every project funds free AI literacy workshops for underserved communities — churches, nonprofits, and families who need the tools but can't afford the tuition.

"Profit isn't the opposite of purpose. It's the fuel for it."
